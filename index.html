Overview

A focused practice for boundary, title, and land documentation matters.

SRNM Consulting Inc. is structured around work that benefits from measured judgment rather than volume. The practice focuses on boundary questions, legal descriptions, title and easement review, exhibit plats, and related mapping that can be reviewed clearly by attorneys, architects, engineers, owners, and title or real estate teams.

Some assignments begin with a preliminary title report. Others begin with a deed, a prior survey, a site plan, or a question about where a line, easement, or access right may fall on the ground. In each case, the goal is the same: prepare work that is understandable, technically grounded, and useful to the matter at hand.

Services

Core services

Services are centered on record research, analysis, drafting, and mapping that support land, title, and project coordination matters.

Boundary Review and Analysis

Review of deeds, maps, adjoiner references, occupation evidence, monumentation, and other available materials to evaluate boundary location issues and support a defensible survey opinion.

Legal Descriptions

Preparation and review of legal descriptions for fee interests, easements, rights of way, dedications, vacations, and related land matters, with emphasis on clarity, consistency, and locatability.

Title Report and Easement Review

Review of preliminary title materials, Schedule B items, recorded easements, exceptions, and related documents to identify what can be mapped, what requires clarification, and what should be carried forward in notes or exhibits.

Exhibit Plats and Parcel Mapping

Preparation of exhibit plats, title exhibits, parcel graphics, right of way figures, and reviewable mapping for reports, internal review, negotiations, and project coordination.

Survey-related support for attorneys, architects, engineers, owners, developers, and title teams dealing with access, encroachments, easements, boundary conditions, or survey deliverables affecting a site.

Approach

Work that starts with the record and stays accountable to the evidence.

Record research before drafting

Deeds, maps, title materials, adjoining references, and available public records are reviewed before conclusions are reduced to a description or exhibit.

Evidence matters

Boundary work is not treated as a drafting exercise alone. Monumentation, occupation, record ties, and the relationship between record and field evidence are considered together.

Clear written work

Descriptions and notes should be readable, reviewable, and consistent with the controlling record. The language should help others follow the work, not make it harder to interpret.

Mapped deliverables that can be used

Exhibits and survey graphics are prepared to communicate land information plainly for review, coordination, permitting, negotiation, or internal decision-making.

Selective scope acceptance

Not every matter is a fit for every schedule or project condition. Work is accepted carefully so it can receive the level of research, drafting, and review it requires.

California practice context

The practice is grounded in California land surveying work, including legal descriptions, title and easement mapping, record of survey awareness, and project support for land and title matters.
