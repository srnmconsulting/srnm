Firm Overview

A focused surveying practice built around careful review, clear writing, and usable mapping.

SRNM Consulting Inc. is a California land surveying practice serving clients who need careful review of boundary, title, easement, and land documentation issues. The work is focused on matters that benefit from sound record research, clear drafting, and disciplined preparation of written and mapped deliverables.

This practice is structured around boundary review, legal descriptions, exhibit plats, title-based mapping, and selective survey support where the work can be approached responsibly and explained clearly.

Services

Services offered

Services are focused on work that benefits from careful record reading, boundary understanding, and well-prepared written or mapped deliverables.

Boundary Analysis

Review of deeds, maps, adjoiner records, occupation evidence, and available monumentation to evaluate boundary location issues and support defensible conclusions.

Legal Descriptions

Preparation and review of legal descriptions for fee interests, easements, rights-of-way, dedications, and related land matters, with emphasis on clarity, consistency, and locatability.

Exhibit Plats and Mapping

Preparation of survey exhibits, easement exhibits, parcel graphics, and title-based maps for reports, internal review, design coordination, and transactional support.

Title Report and Easement Review

Review of preliminary title materials, Schedule B items, recorded easements, exceptions, and related documents to identify what can be mapped, what requires clarification, and what should be noted.

Commercial Due Diligence Support

Support for attorneys, architects, engineers, owners, and real estate teams who need help understanding boundary, access, easement, title, and survey-related issues affecting a site.

Selective Survey Support

Select boundary, topographic, and ALTA-related survey support may be offered depending on scope, location, schedule, and project fit.

Qualifications

Approach and practice standards

Record Research Before Conclusions

Boundary and title-related work is approached through review of the record, available evidence, and the relationship between the two.

Clear Written Work

Legal descriptions, notes, and exhibits should read clearly, fit the record, and be prepared in a form that can be reviewed and followed.

Usable Mapping

Survey graphics and title-based exhibits are prepared to communicate land information plainly for review, coordination, and decision-making.

Selective Scope Acceptance

Work is accepted carefully so that each matter can receive appropriate attention to research, drafting, and review.
