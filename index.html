Services

Focused, high-judgment support for boundary and title-sensitive matters

SRNM Consulting is structured around non-field, consulting-first services that help clients understand risk, improve coordination, and move forward with clarity before costly legal, design, or acquisition decisions are made.

Boundary Analysis & Record Research

Review of deeds, maps, record documents, historical evidence, and boundary conflicts to support defensible professional conclusions and better project decisions.

Legal Descriptions & Exhibit Plats

Preparation of metes and bounds descriptions, easement descriptions, fee takings, and exhibit plats written for clarity, consistency, and professional defensibility.

Title, Easement & Encumbrance Review

Technical review of title reports, Schedule B items, easements, rights of way, and record exceptions to help identify constraints, gaps, and issues early.

Commercial Due Diligence Support

Consulting for acquisition, redevelopment, entitlement, and pre-design matters where boundary, title, or survey risk needs to be understood before teams proceed.

ALTA / Survey Scope Review

Support with ALTA scope interpretation, title-driven survey coordination, and technical review for commercially relevant projects and transaction-related needs.

Survey QA & Technical Review

Independent review of third-party descriptions, exhibits, survey documents, and mapping packages for clarity, consistency, and technical alignment.

About

New firm. Experienced principal. Selective scope.

SRNM Consulting Inc. is a California land surveying and boundary consulting practice focused on title-sensitive, commercially relevant, and technically complex property matters.

The firm is led by a California Professional Land Surveyor with a background in geomatics and surveying, commercial and right-of-way related work, private and public-sector project experience, and an MBA from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. Additional credentials include FAA Part 107 remote pilot certification.

Experience spans California and Chicago, with work informed by both technical surveying practice and business-minded project judgment.
