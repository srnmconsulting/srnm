Qualifications

Approach and practice standards

Land surveying in California is regulated because it affects property rights and the public welfare. Work involving boundary location, legal descriptions, easements, maps, and related land records should be handled with clear professional responsibility and careful review of the available evidence.

Good surveying documents are not improved by extra language. They are improved by accurate research, precise vocabulary, sound judgment, and a format that can be followed and checked.

  • Record research before conclusions
  • Attention to boundary law principles and evidence
  • Clear distinction between record information and field evidence
  • Written and mapped deliverables prepared for review and use
  • Careful coordination where title, design, or legal teams are involved
